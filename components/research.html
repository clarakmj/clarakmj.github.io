Research Assistant

Category: Work Experience

About: I worked as a Research Assistant at the University of Calgary alongside Dr. C. Mannion for about 3 years. I worked on a project that dealt with the phenomenon of pica, the ingestion of non-food items such as clay or ice, among pregnant or breastfeeding women.

The reason I was drawn to this study, other than being in a healthcare field, was because I was fascinated by all the numerous changes that came with pregnancy and postpartum. I also realized, from learning more about pica, that it could cause negative physiological impacts on the mother and baby. The topic involved both body and mind, making it an intriguing research topic.

I was involved throughout the entire span of the project from recruiting participants, to interviewing them, to transcribing data, to analysing data, and to writing up draft papers for publication. Pica is a stigmatized topic, which made recruitment challenging. We ended up recruiting about 13 participants as we expanded the search online over social media sites. I ended up making a Facebook group that allowed pica participants to safely discuss their use of pica without any shame or judgment. This seemed to be a better approach as women felt safe behind the anonymity of their pseudonames online. Publication is pending, as I finished writing up the draft, but awaiting approval for publication from my supervisor.
