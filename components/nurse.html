Perinatal Registered Nurse

Category: Work Experience

About: I worked as a maternity nurse at Surrey Memorial Hospital for over 3 years. I began my nursing career working with postpartum patients and newborns. I worked my way up from working with low-risk healthy postpartum patients to high-risk patients managing diabetes, hypertension, infection, etc.

Then, I completed my BCIT Perinatal Specialty certificate program which allowed me to work full-scope on the unit as a Labor and Delivery nurse. I began seeing intrapartum and antepartum patients, dealing with pre-eclampsia, substance use, premature labor, etc. I often filled in roles such as preceptoring nursing students or orientating new hires.

Although my time as an RN is in the past, the skills that I obtained will last me a lifetime. I learned to be a strong, consistent communicator, as communication is vital to maintaining safety of the patients and order of the unit. I often worked one-on-one with the patients and their families very closely, which meant I was independent and often making urgent calls by myself. But working in a big hospital such as Surrey Memorial Hospital meant I worked with a diverse healthcare team, such as physicians, general practitioners, social workers, pharmacists, dieticians, occupational therapists, etc. I also became a team player by learning when to advocate for patients, when to listen, and when to speak up. Since our unit was always busy, I learned to manage my time efficiently and learn how to manage a heavy workload. This taught me how to prioritize tasks based on urgency and importance.
